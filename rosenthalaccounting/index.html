About

Storyteller.
Scholar. Voice for the Silenced.

"Stories carry greater messages for all of us — it is essential to speak up for those who have been silenced."

Dr. Roni Rosenthal is an award-winning author, writer, and Language and Literature Professor at the University of Maryland system. She has dedicated her career to recovering and amplifying the voices of Jewish communities whose histories are at risk of being forgotten.

Her scholarly work explores the intersection of Holocaust history, Jewish cultural understanding, and the emerging role of artificial intelligence as a tool for historical preservation and sociopolitical analysis. Dr. Rosenthal is a recognized authority on how AI can be responsibly leveraged to deepen our understanding of the Jewish condition — past and present.

A frequent speaker at universities, workshops, and schools across the country, she believes firmly in the power of education, communication, and empowerment as instruments for justice.

Areas of Expertise

Where Memory Meets Modernity

Dr. Rosenthal's work bridges centuries of Jewish heritage with the cutting edge of artificial intelligence — ensuring that the lessons of history remain accessible and relevant in an increasingly digital world.

✡

Holocaust History & Memory

Recovering and contextualizing the lived experiences of Jewish communities across Eastern Europe during the Holocaust — with particular focus on Romanian Jewry under fascist and communist rule. Bringing hidden archives and family testimonies into the historical record.

⬡

AI for Historical Research

A passionate proponent of using artificial intelligence to surface, translate, and interpret historical documents at scale. Dr. Rosenthal examines AI's usability for Jewish cultural scholarship and advocates for ethically designed AI systems that honor minority historical perspectives.

◈

Jewish Cultural Understanding

Bridging contemporary sociopolitical structures with deep Jewish cultural literacy. Her educational seminars and published work foster cross-cultural dialogue, combat antisemitism, and illuminate how Jewish identity has been shaped — and continues to be shaped — by global history.

Where the Lilacs Bloom Once Again

Friddie's Story

Based on the true experiences of a Jewish Romanian family, this award-winning novel follows Friddie Stoleru — a woman unjustly imprisoned for nine years, then condemned to a communist labor camp, who found solace in writing poetry through it all. A tragic, heroic chronicle of resilience, loss, and hope along the Danube under fascist and communist rule.

Poetry · Historical

The Lilacs I Once Knew

Friddie's Poems

Despite the harsh and inhumane conditions of imprisonment, Friddie found solace in writing poetry. These poems — discovered after her death in hundreds of letters to her cousin — speak of joy, loss, fear, resilience, and the indomitable human spirit. Restored, translated, and brought back to life, they are a testament to the power of words as survival.

Speaking & Education

Seminars & Speaking Engagements

Dr. Rosenthal is a dynamic and sought-after speaker at universities, schools, and cultural institutions. Her sessions combine rigorous scholarship with compelling personal narrative, leaving audiences informed, moved, and motivated.

01

Holocaust Memory & Testimony

Personal testimony, archival research, and the moral responsibility of preserving survivor accounts before they are lost to time.

02

AI & Jewish Historical Archives

How large language models and machine learning can unlock untranslated Yiddish, Hebrew, and Ladino documents — and the ethical considerations that must guide this work.

03

Romanian Jewry & the Communist Era

A deep dive into the specific persecution of Jewish communities under Romanian fascism and communism — an often-overlooked chapter of twentieth-century Jewish history.

04

The Jewish Condition in Contemporary Geopolitics

Bridging historical Jewish sociopolitical experience with today's global landscape — antisemitism, identity, resilience, and the ongoing struggle for recognition and safety.

05

The Power of Narrative in Survival

How storytelling, poetry, and letter-writing functioned as acts of resistance and psychological survival in labor camps and prisons — and what they teach us about human resilience.
