About Dr. Rosenthal

Storyteller.
Scholar. Voice for the Silenced.

"Stories carry greater messages for all of us — it is essential to speak up for those who have been silenced."

Dr. Roni Rosenthal is an award-winning author, writer, and Language and Literature Professor at the University of Maryland system. She has dedicated her career to recovering and amplifying the voices of Jewish communities whose histories are at risk of being forgotten — particularly those of Romanian Jewry under fascist and communist rule.

Her scholarly work explores the intersection of Holocaust history, Jewish cultural understanding, and the emerging role of artificial intelligence as a tool for historical preservation and sociopolitical analysis. Dr. Rosenthal is a recognized authority on how AI can be responsibly leveraged to deepen our understanding of the Jewish condition — past and present.

A mother, reader, dancer, genealogy advocate, and frequent speaker at universities and cultural institutions across the country, she believes firmly in the power of education, communication, and empowerment as instruments for justice.

Memoir · Historical Fiction · World War II

Where the Lilacs Bloom Once Again

Friddie's Story

Based on the true experiences of a Jewish Romanian family, this award-winning novel follows Friddie Stoleru — born 1919 in Bucharest — who at 18 married a charming scientist named Freddy, only to see him vanish two weeks later while she was unjustly accused of a crime she never committed.

She spent nine years in a Romanian prison, followed by four years in a communist labour camp. Despite these conditions, Friddie found solace in poetry. Alongside the stories of her Aunt Rosa, Uncle David, and cousins who uprooted their lives to start again in Israel, this is an unforgettable chronicle of resilience, family, and hope along the Danube.

Poetry · Historical · Holocaust Literature

The Lilacs I Once Knew

Friddie's Poems

Despite the harsh and inhumane conditions of imprisonment and forced labour, Friddie Stoleru found solace in writing poetry. These poems, discovered after her death in hundreds of letters and postcards shared with her cousin Aaurica, speak of joy, loss, fear, resilience, and the indomitable human spirit.

Restored, translated, and brought back to life by her family, these verses stand as a testament to the power of words as survival — a remarkable literary companion to Where the Lilacs Bloom Once Again.
